Original PlayStation (1994)

The original PlayStation, released in 1994, revolutionized the gaming industry by introducing CD-ROM technology, which allowed for more complex and expansive games compared to the cartridge-based systems of its time. Its success established Sony as a major player in the gaming market and set new standards for game design and graphics, influencing future consoles. Additionally, the PlayStation's diverse library of titles and strong third-party support helped to legitimize video gaming as a mainstream entertainment medium.
